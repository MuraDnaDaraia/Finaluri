Till Lindemann (born 4 January 1963) is a German singer and songwriter, best known as the lead vocalist of Rammstein and his solo project, Lindemann. Rammstein has sold over 25 million records worldwide, and Lindemann has been named among the 50 Greatest Metal Frontmen by Roadrunner Records. Early Life Lindemann was born in Leipzig, East Germany, and grew up in Wendisch-Rambow. He was a competitive swimmer in his youth but left sports due to an injury. He worked various jobs, including as a carpenter and a basket weaver. In the 1980s, Lindemann played drums for the band First Arsch and later joined Feeling B. In 1994, he co-founded Rammstein, known for its pyrotechnic-heavy performances. Lindemann became a certified pyrotechnician after an onstage accident in 1996. Despite controversies, Rammstein achieved significant success. He launched the Lindemann project in 2015, releasing two albums. Lindemann has published poetry books and appeared in minor film roles. He and Rammstein were featured in David Lynch's Lost Highway Lindemann has four children and is an atheist. He prefers a quiet life in a small village in Mecklenburg-Vorpommern.

Richard Kruspe (born 24 June 1967) is a German musician and guitarist, best known as the lead guitarist of Rammstein and founder of the project Emigrate. Kruspe was born Sven Kruspe in Wittenberge, East Germany. He changed his first name to Richard, believing in the right to change one's identity. His parents divorced early, and he had a challenging relationship with his stepfather. Kruspe took up wrestling for seven years during his youth. At 16, a trip to Czechoslovakia led him to buy a guitar, which inspired him to learn to play. He later studied jazz guitar at the conservatorium in Schwerin. Kruspe moved to East Berlin in the late 1980s. After the fall of the Berlin Wall, he returned to Schwerin and founded several bands, including Das Elegante Chaos and Orgasm Death Gimmick. In 1993, he started a project called Tempelprayers with Oliver Riedel and Christoph Schneider. They later formed Rammstein with Till Lindemann, Paul Landers, and Christian Lorenz, winning a Berlin Senate Metrobeat contest in 1994 Kruspe has three children. He married South African actress Caron Bernstein in 1999 and moved to New York in 2001. They separated in 2004, and he returned to Berlin in 2011. Kruspe has endorsed ESP Guitars since 1996 and primarily uses their models. He has his own signature models, the ESP RZK-I and RZK-II. He also uses Mesa/Boogie amplifiers, Kemper Profiler, and has a signature pickup set with Fishman.
